Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian dish with a rich meat sauce served over spaghetti noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. spaghetti
  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz.) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1/4 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Saut√© Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion, minced garlic, grated carrot, and diced celery. Cook until vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Brown Ground Beef: Push vegetables to the side of the skillet and add ground beef. Cook until browned and no longer pink, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  4. Add Sauce Ingredients: Stir in crushed tomatoes, beef broth, and red wine (if using). Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  5. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Add cooked spaghetti to the skillet with the sauce. Toss well to combine and coat the pasta with the sauce.
  6. Serve: Divide into plates. Garnish with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves.
